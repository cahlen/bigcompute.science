British mathematician and philosopher who founded Ramsey theory at age 25 with a single paper, then died at 26 of hepatitis. His 1930 theorem — that sufficiently large structures must contain ordered substructures — launched an entire field. The question R(5,5)=? remains open nearly a century later.
